# Unit Economics

## What a Converted Lead Is Worth to Credex

Credex sells discounted AI infrastructure credits. Assumptions:
- Average deal size: $2,000 (conservative — a 10-person team switching Cursor + Claude = ~$200/mo credits, 10-month initial purchase)
- Gross margin on credits: ~20% (buying discounted, reselling at discount to retail)
- Contribution per deal: $400
- LTV if customer repurchases twice/year for 2 years: $1,600 contribution

**Conservative converted lead value: $400 first purchase**
**LTV-based lead value: $1,600**

Audit completion → email capture: ~30% conversion
Email capture → Credex consultation booked: ~5% (high-savings users only)
Consultation → credit purchase: ~40%

Overall funnel: 100 audits → 30 emails → 1.5 consultations → 0.6 purchases

**Blended CAC per purchase:** ~$25-50 (time cost only, $0 paid spend)
**Contribution per purchase:** $400
**Payback:** Immediate

## Path to $1M ARR in 18 Months

$1M ARR = $83,333/month revenue to Credex

At $2,000 average deal and 2 purchases/year:
Need ~500 active customers spending ~$4,000/year

Month 1-3: 
- Launch, reach 2,000 audit completions/month
- 600 email captures/month
- 30 consultations/month booked
- 12 purchases/month × $2,000 = $24,000 MRR equivalent (annualized: $288k ARR)

Month 4-9:
- SEO starts compounding (comparison keywords)
- Word of mouth from happy customers
- 5,000 audits/month, 1,500 emails, 75 consultations, 30 purchases/month
- 30 × $2,000 = $60,000/month (annualized: $720k ARR)

Month 10-18:
- Referral program live
- Partnership with 2-3 accelerators driving warm leads
- 10,000 audits/month, 3,000 emails, 150 consultations, 60 purchases/month
- 60 × $2,000 = $120,000/month → $1.44M ARR ✓

**What must be true for this to work:**
1. Audit completion rate stays above 20% (tool must be genuinely useful, not spammy)
2. High-savings users (>$500/mo) are 15%+ of completions (pricing data shows this is realistic for teams of 5+)
3. Credex consultation booking flow is frictionless (Calendly embed, not a form)
4. Credit discount is real and verifiable (users will Google it)
# ECONOMICS

If Credex deployed this tomorrow, the economics can work if a lead is worth enough and the funnel stays efficient. A reasonable estimate is that a converted lead is worth $400-$1,500 in gross profit depending on the size of the AI spend the audit uncovers. A small startup that buys a few hundred dollars of discounted credits may not be worth much individually, but a larger team with recurring spend can become a strong account.

CAC by channel is low if the channel is operator communities and direct outreach. HN and Indie Hackers are close to $0 cash CAC but have high time cost and unpredictable conversion. Targeted founder DMs likely cost $10-$40 of labor per qualified audit if personalized. X threads and community posts can be near $0 cash CAC but are hard to scale without proof.

A profitable funnel might look like this: 20% of completed audits capture email, 25% of captured leads book a Credex consultation, and 15% of booked calls convert into a credit purchase. If the average gross profit per purchase is $300-$600, the math works once the tool is getting enough high-savings audits.

To drive $1M ARR in 18 months, Credex would need either a high volume of medium-size customers or a smaller number of teams with very large AI spend. A rough path is 1,000 customers at $1,000 ARR each, or 500 customers at $2,000 ARR each. That implies strong product-qualified lead conversion and a repeatable procurement motion.
